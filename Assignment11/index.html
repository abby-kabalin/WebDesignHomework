Review: I recently rewatched Inception on a Sunday and I remembered how much I loved this movie. I first watched this movie my Freshman year of college when I was deathly ill. I had stayed at my grandparents house in Lolo because I was stuck in my dorm with no cold meds. They were out of town so I crashed on the downstairs couch and watched Inception and Notorious Basterds. What really stuck out to me was the unique concept and plot line. Breaking into a mind for information instead of a bank or intelligence agency was a brilliant idea and the exectution was well-rounded. The ambiguous ending really had me thinking and in my opinion, he was still in Limbo when the screen went black. As far as casting and special effects go, this movie really outdid itself. I've watched Marvel movies with double Inception's $160 million budget and they don't even come close to the quality of the SFX. I love movies that I can just be in awe of the SFX instead of thinking "woah there's the SFX". Special effects should enhance immersion, not pull the watcher out of it. Casting was spot on as well, kudos to Luise Eigner & John Papsidera, I really couldn't imagine the movie without Leonardo, Joseph, Elliot, Tom or Cillian. Also as a side note, absolute props to Joseph and Tom. Their chemistry was amazing and even though they had few interactions, they showed that they had previously worked together and seemed to be friends before /something/ happened. It's not often that you get standalone stories that the ability to make you question what happened without explicitly stating or showing a flashback. These two and Leo's character perfectly encapsulated the idea of "show, don't tell". I could go on and on about this movie but I'll leave it at this: Inception is a masterclass in storytelling, casting, and special effects. I would recommend this movie to anyone who loves a good thriller or a movie that makes you think. I personally love movies and shows that leave you googling after the credits role. Inception is a movie that will have you thinking about it for days after you watch it.
